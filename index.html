History of Dagda

Dagda is a town in Krāslava municipality in Latgale, Latvia . It is located on the eastern slope of the Latgalian Uplands , by Lake Dagda and the Narūta River, 36 km northeast of Krāslava , 267 km from Riga . It was the center of the former Dagda district (1950-1962) and Dagda municipality (2009-2021).Dagda manor and a village of small traders and craftsmen are mentioned in historical sources during the existence of the Inflant Voivodeship in the 17th century. In 1705, the Viļaka starast Juris Hilzens built the Dagda Catholic Church, in 1741, the father of the writer Jozef Hilzens Jans Augusts Hilzens (1702-1767) built the current Dagda Holy Trinity Church . From 1742 to 1820, a Jesuit mission operated in Dagda
